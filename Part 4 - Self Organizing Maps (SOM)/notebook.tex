\paragraph{How SOMs work:}\label{how-soms-work}}

They basically reduce the dimentionality. take multidimentional
dataset---\textgreater{}reduce the dimentions by representing it with
the maps. columns reduced by 2d map.

\texttt{SOM} depicting different states of prosperity and poverty in
different countries(based on lots of different properties). As it is a
type of unsupervised learning, it puts similar countries together.

    \hypertarget{how-soms-learn-kohonen-algo}{%
\paragraph{How SOMs learn: KOHONEN
algo}\label{how-soms-learn-kohonen-algo}}

For every row we find the \texttt{BMU}(updating the weights and finding
the least distance between the points selected row and the new
representative point) which is \texttt{Best\ matching\ unit} for the
every nodes near each one(inside the certain fixed radius) weights are
updated.

in the next epoch(iteration) unique feature of KOHONEN algo is applited
the radiuses shrink. \texttt{BMUs} are not pulling as much of other
nodes than before. i.e.~the process becomes more and more
accurate(specific and targeted).

Take aways: SOMs retain topology of the input set SOMs reveal
correlations that are not easily identified. SOMs classify data without
supervision. no target vectors i.e no backpropagation. No lateral
connections between output nodes.
